## 📖 Project Overview
Retail businesses generate large volumes of transactional data every month, but without proper analysis, it's difficult to identify who is buying, what they're buying, and when. Understanding these patterns is key to smarter inventory planning, targeted marketing, and stronger sales performance.

This project analyzes retail transaction data to uncover purchasing patterns across customer demographics, product categories, and time. Using Excel PivotTables, PivotCharts, and slicers, the project transforms raw sales data into an interactive dashboard that supports data-driven retail decisions.

### The dashboard enables users to:
- Monitor total sales performance across product categories
- Analyze purchasing behavior by age group and gender
- Track monthly and seasonal sales trends
- Examine how transaction size (quantity bought) affects revenue
- Support inventory, marketing, and promotional planning

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- Data Processing: Microsoft Excel (Power Query)
- Data Visualization: Excel PivotTables & PivotCharts, Slicers
- Dataset: Retail Transaction Records (customer demographics, product category, quantity, and date of purchase)

| Time | Month & Year of Purchase |

### Age Categories:
Adult, Youth, and Elderly used to evaluate purchasing power and category preference across life stages.

## 🧹 Data Preparation & Feature Engineering
Data preparation was performed using Excel and Power Query. The following steps were applied:
- Imported retail transaction records
- Validated data types for numerical and categorical fields
- Removed duplicate and blank records
- Grouped customers into age categories for analysis
- Built PivotTables to summarize sales by category, gender, age, and month
- Added slicers for interactive filtering by product category and month

## 🔍 Insights & Findings
1. **Adults Are the Highest-Spending Segment** - Adult customers significantly outspend Youth and Elderly customers across nearly every product category.
2. **Category Revenue Is Closely Matched** - Electronics ($156,905), Clothing ($155,580), and Beauty ($143,515) generate similar total revenue, showing a well-balanced product mix rather than a single dominant category.
3. **Sales Are Seasonally Volatile** - Monthly sales fluctuate noticeably, with a sharp dip around January, suggesting a post-holiday slowdown.
4. **Larger Transactions Drive More Revenue** - Purchases involving 4+ items generate substantially more revenue than single-item transactions, highlighting the value of bundling and upselling.
5. **Gender Differences Are Category-Specific** - While overall spending is similar between genders, category preference varies, with some categories showing a stronger pull for one gender over the other.

## 🩺 Recommendations
Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are proposed:

### 1. Target Adult Shoppers
Since Adults drive the majority of sales, marketing budgets and loyalty programs should prioritize this segment.

### 2. Promote Bundling & Upselling
Since larger transactions generate significantly more revenue, retailers should design bundle deals and "buy more, save more" promotions.

### 3. Plan for the January Slowdown
Introduce targeted discounts or campaigns in January to offset the post-holiday dip in sales.

### 4. Tailor Category Marketing by Gender
Use gender-specific product preferences to guide targeted email campaigns and in-store promotions.
